Great Smoky Mountains National Park

Park's History & Significance

Established in 1934 by Congress, the Great Smoky Mountains National Park is known for its biodiversity, mountains, and preserved Appalachian culture and holds the distinction of being the most visited national park in the United States. It is located in Southeastern United States, with parts in both North Carolina and Tennessee. For thousands of years, this region provided home for numerous successive cultures of Indegenous peoples, indluding the Cherokee. European Americans began settling here in the late 18th century and President Andrew Jackson signed the Indian Removal Act that cleared this area of its native tribes. Logging became an enormous industry in this are and was stripping the region of its trees, until the park became protected in the 1930's.

Location & Geography

Straddling the border of North Carolina and Tennessee, the park encompasses the Great Smoky Mountains, part of the Appalachian Mountain range. Its mist-covered peaks and diverse ecosystems make it a unique natural treasure.

Native Flora and Fauna

The park is a haven for biodiversity, hosting a vast array of plant and animal species. The park is 96% forested provides home to over 19,000 known species of organisms, and over 100,000 undocumented species. Over 100 species of trees grow in the park, including deciduous and coniferous, and 5,600 species of plants. Rich forests, wildflowers, and habitats support over 1,500 black bears and numerous elk, bird species, otters, rodents, and so much more, making it a paradise for nature enthusiasts.

Climate & Weather

The Great Smoky Mountains mostly experience a temperate climate. Summers are warm and humid with hot weather in the lowlands and warm weather in the mountian tops. Winters are fairly mild in the lowlands and colder and snowier at higher elevation, while winters can bring snow to higher elevations. Rainfall contributes to the park's lush vegetation, providing 55-100+ inches of precipitation a year.

Visitor Tips and Peak Times

Visit during spring for blooming wildflowers or fall for vibrant foliage. Be sure to research and explore scenic drives, hiking trails, and historic sites. Also, be prepared for changing weather conditions and check for park updates before your visit.
